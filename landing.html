The Gocta Waterfall is amongst the tallest of the world, and is a premier natural attraction of the Amazonas Region. It is over 771 meters tall, and is surrounded by minor falls. Combined, they create an incredible natural pool at its base perfect for you to enjoy!.

El Inti Raymi, la “Fiesta del Sol”, fue una de las más importantes durante el reinado de los Incas. Además de adorar al Sol y agradecerle por las buenas cosechas, se esperaba que los tributos agradaran al Inca. Así, se buscaba persuadirlo para que se quede cerca de la tierra cusqueña y de su familia.

Kuelap is the hottest peruvian tourist destination: it is being thought of as the new Machu Picchu. The city, built as a fort, has an incredible view of the whole valle and contains 12 sites and more than 400 circular houses in reference to immortality.

According to the historical timeline, the  ancient city was built 4,000 years before Machu Picchu.  The Norte Chico Civilization existed around 3500 BC. This means it was built prior the Pyramids of Giza (!!!!)
